How the council is led

Gloucester City Council is led by elected councillors and supported by professional officers. Together, they deliver services and make decisions that affect our city.

Political Leadership

The council is made up of 39 elected councillors who represent the city's 16 wards. Councillors are elected every four years.

The Leader of the Council

The Leader is elected by councillors and is responsible for the council's overall direction and leadership. The Leader appoints the Cabinet.

The Cabinet

The Cabinet is made up of up to 10 councillors, each with responsibility for specific areas of council work. The Cabinet makes most of the council's major decisions.

View Leader and Cabinet

Officer Structure

The council employs professional officers to deliver services and provide advice to councillors.

Chief Executive

The Chief Executive is the head of paid service and leads the council's officers. They are responsible for ensuring the council operates effectively and delivers its priorities.

Corporate Management Team

The Corporate Management Team includes the Chief Executive and senior directors responsible for different service areas.

How Decisions Are Made

The council makes decisions through various committees and meetings:

  • Cabinet - Makes most major decisions
  • Full Council - Makes key decisions like setting the budget
  • Committees - Make decisions on planning, licensing, and scrutiny
