Civic Leadership

Ancient offices dating back over 800 years

The Mayor and Sheriff are ceremonial roles with deep historical roots. The Sheriff was established by King John's Charter in 1200, making it nearly 500 years older than the City Council itself. The first recorded Mayor was Richard the Burgess in 1228.

Political Leadership

Elected by residents

The council is made up of 39 elected councillors who represent the city's 16 wards. Councillors are elected every four years to make decisions on behalf of residents and champion local issues.

üèõÔ∏è The Leader

The Leader is elected by councillors and is responsible for the council's overall direction and leadership. The Leader appoints the Cabinet and represents the council publicly.

üë• The Cabinet

The Cabinet is made up of up to 10 councillors, each with responsibility for specific areas like housing, environment, or finance. They make most major decisions.

All 39 councillors meet regularly as Full Council to make key decisions including setting the budget, agreeing major policies, and appointing committees.

Scrutiny councillors hold the Cabinet to account, review decisions, and investigate issues that matter to residents.

Officer Structure

The council employs around 400 professional officers to deliver services and provide advice to councillors. Officers implement the decisions made by councillors and ensure services run smoothly.

Chief Executive

The Chief Executive is the head of paid service and leads all council officers. They ensure the council operates effectively and delivers on its priorities.

Corporate Management Team

Senior directors responsible for different service areas such as communities, place, and resources. They manage teams and budgets.

Service Managers

Managers lead specific services like planning, housing, environmental services, and customer services.

How Decisions Are Made

The council makes decisions through various meetings and committees:

  • Cabinet - Makes most major decisions on services and policies
  • Full Council - Sets the budget and agrees the policy framework
  • Planning Committee - Decides planning applications
  • Licensing Committee - Makes decisions on licences for taxis, alcohol, and entertainment
  • Overview & Scrutiny - Reviews and challenges Cabinet decisions
  • Audit & Governance - Ensures financial controls and good governance

Public participation

Most council meetings are open to the public. You can attend in person, watch online, or even speak at certain meetings to share your views.
